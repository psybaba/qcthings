The 48-Hour Audit & Analysis

A two-part activity to analyze your time management habits with real data.

Part 1: The 48-Hour Time Audit

Your first task is to become a researcher with yourself as the subject. You will track your time for two full, consecutive days. For the best insights, we recommend choosing one weekday and one weekend day (e.g., Thursday and Friday).

How to Track Your Time:

  • Use a simple notebook, a spreadsheet (Google Sheets/Excel), or a time-tracking app.
  • Log your primary activity in 30-minute intervals.
  • Be honest and non-judgmental. The goal is accurate data, not perfection.

Example Log:

Time Primary Activity Category Notes / Feelings
9:00-9:30 AM Started reading for Psych 101 Academics / Studying Focused
9:30-10:00 AM Got distracted by texts, scrolled Instagram Personal / Scrolling Lost focus, felt anxious

Categories: Academics (Studying/Class), Work, Errands, Chores, Self-Care, Socializing, Personal/Scrolling, Sleep, etc.

Part 2: The Time Audit Analysis Tool

Once your 48-hour audit is complete, use the tool below to analyze your data. For each statement, review your time log and rate yourself based on the evidence you recorded.

1. Planning & Goals

Look at your log. How much of your academic or work time was planned in advance (e.g., "study for bio exam") vs. decided on the spot?

2. Managing Interruptions

Review your study/work blocks. How often were you distracted by things like notifications, social media, or other people?

3. Prioritizing Tasks

Did your time log show that you completed high-priority tasks (like studying for a test tomorrow) *before* low-priority tasks (like reorganizing your desk)?

4. "In-Between" Time

Find any "in-between" time in your log (e.g., waiting for class, commuting, waiting in line). How did you use it?

5. Combating Procrastination

Identify a task you were dreading. Does your log show that you tackled it head-on, or that you filled your time with other, less important activities to avoid it?

6. Balancing Life

Look at the overall distribution of your time across categories (Academics, Work, Social, Self-Care, Sleep). How balanced does it appear to be?

7. Energy Management

Look at your notes/feelings column. Does your log show you did high-focus tasks during your most energetic times of day, or did you try to study when you felt tired?

Final Reflection
